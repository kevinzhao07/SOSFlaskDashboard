Welcome to System for Opioid Overdose Surveilance

What is SOS?

SOS is a near real-time opioid overdose surveillance system created through a partnership between the University of Michigan Injury Prevention Center and Michigan High Intensity Drug Trafficking Areas (HIDTA). SOS provides near real-time mapping of non-fatal and fatal overdose cases, as well as aggregate data briefs, to facilitate timely public health and public safety responses.

What information does SOS Report?

SOS currently collects data from the following sources:

  • Emergency Medical Services (EMS)

    EMS data is obtained through the Michigan EMS Information System (MI-EMSIS) and includes statewide cases of naloxone administration. Naloxone administration may not indicate an opioid overdose, as naloxone is commonly administered by first responders to any unresponsive patient. Naloxone administration incident and city location is displayed on the SOS map as reported by EMS agencies to MI-EMSIS. Not all EMS agencies report incidents on a real-time basis and cases may be under-reported.

  • Medical Examiners (ME)

    Fatal suspected opioid overdose data is obtained through the electronic death database, Medicolegal Death Investigation Log (MDILog) and individual county medical examiners. Suspected overdose cases are determined through death investigators and updated to SOS on a regular basis. As toxicology reports become available, SOS is updated to reflect confirmed opioid related deaths.

Due to the near-real time nature of SOS, all data collected is provisional and subject to change. Data may not always be complete and data should not be considered a definitive opioid overdose. All locations of the map are approximate.

Who has access to SOS?

SOS is available to public health and public safety stakeholders involved in opioid overdose response and prevention efforts. The University of Michigan Injury Prevention Center monitors those accessing SOS to ensure proper use.

Please reach out to SOS.Reports@umich.edu with any questions or to request access.
